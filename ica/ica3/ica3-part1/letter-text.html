Exotic Dance Moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. My favorite dances include:

Polynesian Chicken Dance

A little-known but influential dance dating back to 300 BC. A whole village would dance in a circle like chickens to encourage livestock to be "fruitful."

Icelandic Brownian Shuffle

Before the Icelanders discovered fire, they developed this dance to stay warm. It involved huddling in a circle and shuffling rapidly in tiny, imperceptible movements. Some say this inspired modern styles like Twerking.

Arctic Robot Dance

English explorers in the 1960s mistakenly thought they discovered a "robotic" dance style among Northern Alaskan and Canadian inhabitants. Later, they realized the movements were due to extreme cold.
